Terms of service.

What you can expect from us, and what we ask of you. Read carefully — we will reference these throughout our work.

These terms apply to all services provided by Robora Therapy Solutions. By booking a session, you agree to them. Last updated: April 2026.

Our services

Robora provides counselling, family therapy, and psycho-educational services to children, teenagers, adults, families, schools, and faith communities. Sessions are offered online via secure video conference and in person in Lagos.

Sessions and fees

Standard counselling sessions are 50 minutes long. Initial intake sessions may run up to 75 minutes. Our session fee is NGN 50,000 per session. Family sessions, school programs, and church partnerships are quoted separately based on scope. Fees are payable in advance of each session unless an alternative arrangement has been agreed in writing.

Package rates for ongoing work (4, 8, or 12 sessions) are available at a small discount. Please ask if you would like to discuss this.

Booking and rescheduling

Sessions are booked by mutual agreement after your initial 20-minute call. Once a session is booked, that time is held for you alone — Joanne does not double-book.

If you need to reschedule, please give at least 24 hours' notice. We understand life happens; one or two short-notice changes are met with grace, not penalty.

Cancellations and missed sessions

Cancellations made with more than 24 hours' notice are not charged. Cancellations within 24 hours, or sessions missed without notice, are charged at the full session fee. This is not a punishment — it is how we honour the time held for you.

If you miss two consecutive sessions without communication, we will pause your booking and reach out to check in. If we do not hear back within 14 days, your slot is released to the waitlist.

The work itself

Counselling is not a service you receive passively, like a haircut. It is a relationship. The most meaningful change happens when you bring honesty, willingness, and patience. We will hold up our side: showing up prepared, listening carefully, offering tools that suit your particular story, and never pretending to know what we do not know.

We do not promise outcomes. We promise care, competence, and consistency. The work will surprise you, sometimes in hard ways. That is part of how it heals.

What we are, and what we are not

Robora provides therapy and counselling. We are not a crisis service, an emergency room, or a psychiatric hospital. We do not prescribe medication. We do not provide forensic assessments for legal proceedings unless commissioned separately and in writing.

If you or your child are in immediate danger, please contact emergency services or a crisis line. We will list current Nigerian and international resources on our accessibility page.

Confidentiality

What we discuss in sessions stays in sessions, with specific legal exceptions. Please read our confidentiality notice in full before our first session — it covers limits, mandated reporting, and the special considerations that apply when the client is a minor.

Records

We keep brief written notes of each session, in line with professional standards. These are protected by both ethics and law, and are stored as described in our privacy policy. You may request a copy of your records at any time.

Communication between sessions

Brief logistical messages (rescheduling, asking a quick question) are welcome by email. Therapeutic conversations belong in sessions, not in email — for both our wellbeing and the integrity of the work. If something arises that cannot wait, we will arrange a call.

Endings

Either you or Joanne may end the work at any time, for any reason. Where possible, we ask for a final session to close well — to mark what has been done, name what remains, and part with care.

Complaints

If something in our work falls short, please tell us. The first step is a conversation directly with Joanne. If that does not resolve things, formal complaints can be directed to the relevant professional body, listed on request.

Governing law

These terms are governed by the laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria. Any dispute that cannot be resolved through conversation will be addressed in the courts of Lagos State.

Questions?

If anything is unclear, ask.

Terms documents are necessary but never the whole picture. We would rather answer five clarifying questions before you book than have you wonder once you have started.
