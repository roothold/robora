A note from the founder

The eye of an educator. The heart of a counsellor.

“I started Robora because I realised that no wealth could mask trauma. When children act out or withdraw, it is the pain inside speaking — and that pain has a name, a story, and a way home.”

Robora draws its meaning from the Latin roborare — to strengthen. After more than a decade in classrooms across nations, I trained as a counsellor to help children and families unlock the strength I always saw was there. Like the oak whose roots run deep, every person carries an unspoken resilience waiting to be stirred.

My work is the meeting place of education and emotional healing — a calm, soothing, safe place where families restore connection, and children rise into the truest version of themselves.

— Joanne
CERTIFIED EDUCATOR & FAMILY THERAPIST

How we work together

A path that honours your pace.

Every Robora journey is shaped to the person in front of us. We blend clinical insight with classroom wisdom — and, where it is welcomed, with a quiet thread of faith.

No two stories are the same. But the rhythm of the work tends to look like this:

  1. A free 20-minute call to listen, understand, and name what you are carrying.
  2. An intake session to map history, hopes, and the shape of the road ahead.
  3. A tailored plan — weekly or fortnightly — drawing from trauma-informed and creative therapies.
  4. Family or school check-ins where helpful, so healing is held by more than one set of hands.
  5. A gentle close, with tools you keep, for the seasons that come after.
